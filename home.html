Pygmy Hippo

Pygmy Hippo

The pygmy hippopotamus (Choeropsis liberiensis) is a small hippopotamid which is native to the forests and swamps of West Africa, primarily in Liberia, with small populations in Sierra Leone, Guinea, and Ivory Coast. It has been extirpated from Nigeria. The pygmy hippo is reclusive and nocturnal.

Habitat:

The Pygmy Hippopotamus is found in western Africa, mainly in Liberia, but also in Sierra Leone, Guinea, and Côte d'Ivoire. A vital area for the Pygmy Hippo's protection is Sapo National Park in eastern Liberia.

Diet:

The species is exclusively vegetarian, feeding on leaves and roots of forest plants (including semi-aquatic plants and forest herbs) as well as on fallen fruit.

Pygmy hippos exhibit excellent swimming abilities.

Why are Pygmy Hippo's Important?

Like the common hippopotamus (Hippopotamus amphibius), pygmy hippos play important rile in natural ecosystems by seeking higher quality vegetation and functioning as seed dispersers and nutrient recyclers by spreading dung.

Why are Pygmy Hippo's Endangered?

They are classified as Endangered on the 2010 IUCN Red List of Threatened Species. A distinct pygmy hippo subspecies (C. liberiensis heslopi) that once existed in Nigeria has most probably gone extinct. These declines result from habitat loss and hunting.
